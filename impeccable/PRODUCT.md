# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective collaborators, clients, teachers, admissions reviewers, and hiring contacts who need to quickly understand Adam Szilagyi's video production range. They arrive with limited time, often scanning on mobile, and want proof of editing skill, camera work, storytelling judgment, and reliability before deciding whether to watch a full piece, review the CV, or make contact.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio showcases Adam's video editing, cinematography, and multimedia production work across informational, freeform, and news-package categories. It exists to turn a collection of school, client, documentary, and broadcast-style projects into a clear professional signal: Adam can plan, shoot, edit, interview, and present video work with purpose. Success means visitors can understand the categories, sample the best work, recognize the tools used, and reach out without friction.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, capable, story-minded. The voice should feel like a young creative with real production experience: sincere, direct, observant, and proud of the work without overselling it. The emotional goal is quiet confidence, the feeling that each project has a human reason for existing.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic dark portfolio templates that feel interchangeable. Avoid overproduced agency theatrics, neon creator aesthetics, heavy glassmorphism, resume-only pages, and effects that compete with the actual videos. Avoid design patterns that make the work feel like filler around a layout instead of the central evidence.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the work. Thumbnails, titles, and project context should carry the experience before decorative design does.
2. Keep the story attached to the artifact. Each video should explain what Adam made, who it served, and what production skills were involved.
3. Make skill visible without turning the site into a scorecard. Tool tags and proficiency markers should support credibility, not dominate the page.
4. Build confidence through clarity. Navigation, categories, CV access, and contact should stay obvious on every screen.
5. Let the portfolio feel human. The site should preserve Adam's student-creator voice while polishing grammar, hierarchy, and presentation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline. Preserve semantic HTML, visible keyboard focus, descriptive alt text, readable contrast, and touch-friendly controls. Motion should be subtle, nonessential, and disabled for users who prefer reduced motion. Video links should make their destination and content clear before opening a new tab.
